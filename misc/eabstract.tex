% Abstract of the thesis in English
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

For the practical deployment of autonomous driving systems, high levels of safety and adaptability are essential.
Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL), which allows an agent to learn driving strategies through interaction with the environment, has attracted significant attention.
However, DRL can raise safety concerns when applied in real-world scenarios due to challenges such as reward design and poor generalization to out-of-distribution situations.
To address these issues, Constrained Reinforcement Learning (CRL) has been proposed, which seeks to balance safety and performance during policy learning.
CRL is typically formulated using Constrained Markov Decision Processes (CMDPs), where constraints are defined as expected cumulative costs.
Nevertheless, in autonomous driving, some constraints—such as collision avoidance—require immediate reactions.
Thus, even when CMDP-based policies satisfy cumulative cost-based constraints, violations of instant constraints may still occur.
In this paper, we propose a Lagrangian-based policy optimization method that estimates state-wise Lagrangian multipliers to precisely reflect constraints and consider state-level safety.
The proposed method is validated on a navigation task in the Safety Gym environment developed by OpenAI, where a vehicle must reach a designated goal location.
